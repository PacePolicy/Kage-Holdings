Mark Carney Campaign Promises

The Liberal Party promises to do the following:

  • Reduce internal trade costs by up to 15%
  • Expand economy by up to $200 billion (about $5,000 per Canadian)
  • Table legislation by Canada Day to eliminate all federal barriers to interprovincial trade and labour mobility
  • Achieve mutual recognition of professional credentials across provinces
  • Mandate federal departments to review and report within 60 days on regulatory streamlining
  • Prioritize major economic projects that span provincial/territorial boundaries

Mark Carney’s government will invest in large-scale infrastructure to physically and economically unite Canada, strengthen our sovereignty, and power long-term growth through clean energy and trade corridors.

  • Support projects like: Port of Churchill, Edmonton hydrogen, Cowessess solar, Windsor–Québec City high-speed rail, Grays Bay corridor, George Massey Tunnel, and Trans-Canada twinning.
  • Make the Confederation Bridge, Marine Atlantic, and other ferries more affordable, especially for PEI residents.
  • Invest in a new $5 billion Trade Diversification Corridors Fund for ports, rail, highways, and airports.
  • Double the Indigenous Loan Guarantee Program from $5B to $10B, expanding access to major project ownership.
  • Expand east-west electricity grid to connect clean power across Canada and strengthen energy security.
  • Add $250 million to the Rural Transit Solutions Fund for local and regional transit.
  • Invest in AI infrastructure, rural broadband, and reliable cell service to boost innovation and connectivity.
  • Provide disaster recovery funds to restore infrastructure affected by climate disasters.
  • Support building and upgrading health facilities, community infrastructure, and accessible housing.

Mark Carney’s team will streamline approvals, boost project coordination, and remove roadblocks that slow down essential infrastructure while upholding environmental and Indigenous rights.

  • Cut the average project approval time by 50% without compromising quality or consultation.
  • Coordinate permitting across levels of government to eliminate duplication and delays.
  • Strengthen Indigenous consultation by setting clear timelines, supporting capacity, and improving data-sharing.
  • Launch a National Permitting Coordinator to manage and monitor progress on major projects.
  • Create a Major Projects Leadership Team to work directly with proponents and communities.
  • Establish service standards and performance metrics to track permitting effectiveness.
  • Use digital platforms to track applications, status updates, and transparency in reviews.
  • Modernize impact assessment laws to provide clarity and reduce uncertainty.

A Mark Carney-led government will unlock investment to support housing and infrastructure by coordinating public and private capital more effectively.

  • Leverage Canada Infrastructure Bank to unlock $100 billion+ in private capital for public-interest projects.
  • Double the Indigenous Loan Guarantee Program from $5B to $10B and expand eligible sectors.
  • Finance major housing developments with federal land, low-cost loans, and tax incentives.
  • Create a national pipeline of projects to provide clear opportunities for long-term investment.
  • Use public-private partnerships (P3s) for transit, green infrastructure, and clean energy transmission.
  • Offer credit enhancement tools to lower borrowing costs for municipalities and Indigenous governments.
  • Modernize procurement and financing rules to prioritize shovel-ready projects that meet climate and housing goals.
