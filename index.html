Mark Carney Campaign Promises

The Liberal Party promises to do the following:

  • Reduce internal trade costs by up to 15%
  • Expand the economy by up to $200 billion (about $5,000 per Canadian)
  • Table legislation by Canada Day to eliminate all federal barriers to interprovincial trade and labour mobility
  • Achieve mutual recognition of professional credentials across provinces
  • Mandate federal departments to review and report within 60 days on regulatory streamlining
  • Prioritize major economic projects that span provincial/territorial boundaries

Mark Carney’s government will invest in large-scale infrastructure to unite Canada, strengthen sovereignty, and promote long-term growth through clean energy and trade corridors.

  • Support projects like Port of Churchill, Edmonton hydrogen, and high-speed rail from Windsor to Québec City
  • Make the Confederation Bridge and Marine Atlantic more affordable for PEI residents
  • Invest in a new $5 billion Trade Diversification Corridors Fund for ports, rail, highways, and airports
  • Double the Indigenous Loan Guarantee Program from $5B to $10B, expanding access to major project ownership
  • Expand the east-west electricity grid to connect clean power across Canada and enhance energy security
  • Provide an additional $250 million to the Rural Transit Solutions Fund for local and regional transit

Mark Carney’s team will streamline approvals, boost project coordination, and remove roadblocks that slow down essential infrastructure while upholding environmental and Indigenous rights.

  • Cut the average project approval time by 50% without compromising quality or consultation
  • Coordinate permitting across levels of government to eliminate duplication and delays
  • Strengthen Indigenous consultation by setting clear timelines, supporting capacity, and improving data-sharing
  • Launch a National Permitting Coordinator to manage and monitor progress on major projects
  • Create a Major Projects Leadership Team to work directly with proponents and communities
  • Establish service standards and performance metrics to track permitting effectiveness
  • Use digital platforms to track applications, status updates, and transparency in reviews
  • Modernize impact assessment laws to provide clarity and reduce uncertainty

A Mark Carney-led government will unlock investment to support housing and infrastructure by coordinating public and private capital more effectively.

  • Leverage Canada Infrastructure Bank to unlock $100 billion+ in private capital for public-interest projects
  • Double the Indigenous Loan Guarantee Program from $5B to $10B and expand eligible sectors
  • Finance major housing developments with federal land, low-cost loans, and tax incentives
  • Create a national pipeline of projects to provide clear opportunities for long-term investment
  • Use public-private partnerships (P3s) for transit, green infrastructure, and clean energy transmission
  • Offer credit enhancement tools to lower borrowing costs for municipalities and Indigenous governments
  • Modernize procurement and financing rules to prioritize shovel-ready projects that meet climate and housing goals

A Mark Carney-led government will protect and strengthen CBC/Radio-Canada to ensure Canadian stories are told in a time of rising external threats.

  • Introduce legislation to establish statutory funding for CBC/Radio-Canada
  • Increase funding by $150 million annually and develop a strategic plan to meet new mandates
  • Support CBC/Radio-Canada’s innovation with new digital tools and improved citizen engagement
  • Strengthen the local news mandate to improve access to timely and reliable news across the country
  • Ensure representation of Indigenous perspectives in all CBC/Radio-Canada programming
  • Equip CBC/Radio-Canada to combat disinformation and ensure Canadians trust their news source
  • Enhance funding to support Canadian creators and artists, including Canada Council for the Arts and Telefilm

A Mark Carney-led government will encourage Canadians to discover and rediscover the beauty of Canada through free and discounted access to national parks and galleries.

  • Introduce the Canada Strong Pass for children and youth under 18 with free access to national galleries and museums
  • Provide free access to National Parks and Historic Sites for everyone during the summer months
  • Reduce prices for camping sites in national parks from June to August for all Canadians
  • Offer discounted access and fares for young Canadians aged 18-24 to encourage travel within Canada

A Mark Carney-led government will prioritize Canadian businesses and products to strengthen the domestic economy in the face of trade challenges.

  • Increase transparency and stringency in origin of product labelling to make it easier for Canadians to identify truly Canadian products
  • Deploy a made-in-Canada procurement strategy that prioritizes Canadian suppliers and supply chains
  • Prioritize Canadian contractors in defence procurement, including the Canadian aerospace industry
  • Ensure Government of Canada purchases include low carbon emissions and sustainability-certified products
  • Maximize the use of Canadian steel, aluminum, and forestry products for federal infrastructure projects
  • Encourage the purchase of Canadian-built vehicles to support the auto industry and drive domestic investment
  • Leverage government funding to support Canadian innovators and entrepreneurs through government purchases
